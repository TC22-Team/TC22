IAPR TC-22 Terms of Reference (ToR)

This document is the IAPR TC-22 Terms of Reference (ToR), which includes general guidelines about the activity of the group, as well as specific rules.

  1. Introduction
    1. TC-22 is an official Technical Committee of the International Association for Pattern Recognition (IAPR). The goal of TC-22 is to promote and support reproducible practices within the pattern recognition community. This includes fostering tools, platforms, and standards that make it easier for researchers to share code, data, and results in a transparent and verifiable way. Although TC-22 has a special focus on Pattern Recognition, with regard to reproducibility, it includes all other research domains represented by the other IAPR TCs.
    2. The official website of TC-22 is at https://iapr-tc22.org/, which includes a link of these ToR at https://iapr-tc22.org/ToR.html.
  2. Joining TC-22 as a member
    1. Researchers interested in reproducibility or pattern recognition are very welcome to join TC-22.
    2. Candidates need to send an email to the Board with a short statement explaining their interest in reproducible research or pattern recognition, and how they plan to contribute to TC-22
    3. The members are expected to vote for initiatives proposed by the Board, as well as proposing activities, or contributing to ongoing ones.
    4. New members need to accept that at least their name and affiliation appears in the TC-22 website. Optionally, with a link to their website.
    5. Membership is by default permanent, unless the member expresses his or her wish to leave TC-22.
    6. TC-22 membership might be terminated if the Board deems that the conduct or activity of a member is not compatible with the IAPR’s Statement of Ethics and Policy on Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion (https://iapr.org/about-us/constitution-and-bylaws/statement-of-ethics-and-policy-on-equality-diversity-and-inclusion/)
      1. The board decides if a termination is permanent or whether a candidate might re-apply in the future.
  3. The TC-22 board
    1. The Board is the decision-making body of TC-22
    2. It includes a Main Chair, a Vice-Chair, and several officers in charge of diverse areas.
    3. The existing areas are at this moment:
      1. Workshop coordination
      2. GitHub team management and animation
      3. RR resources
      4. Coordination with other IAPR TCs
      5. Editorial RR diffusion
    4. The Board needs to be as balanced and representative as possible, including diversity in gender, geographical location, and research field, among others. Note that the research field must be related to Reproducible Research or Pattern Recognition.
    5. The Main Chair is appointed by the IAPR President. Please refer to the By-Laws IAPR document for the chairing period and other details: https://iapr.org/about-us/constitution-and-bylaws/by-laws/.
    6. The Main Chair and the Vice-Chair of TC-22
      1. The Main Chair and the vice-Chair are authorized to speak in the name of TC-22 and serve as the contact point.
      2. Election of Chairs.
        1. They are members of the Board
        2. The IAPR President will designate a Main Chair among the Board. The Board may suggest names, although these suggestions are totally non-binding.
        3. The election process will be organized by the exiting Main Chair and Vice-Chair.
      3. Responsibilities of the Main Chair / Vice-chair
        1. Keep track of the TC-22 activities and reporting to IAPR.
        2. Organize regular meetings with the Board. They decide which is the optimal regularity.
        3. Proposing the RRPR workshop.
        4. Serve as a liaison between TC-22 and any other 3rd parties. They are the main contact point.
        5. The Chairs can arrange a meeting with the Board or all the members whenever is needed.
        6. The Chairs need to gather the Board to discuss and reach consensus for major decisions. If the issue is not of major importance, the Chairs can take action directly, for agility reasons.
    7. Who can apply to become a member of the Board?
      1. A member of TC-22 who is interested in a particular area can apply to assume a responsibility as an officer. Please refer to the Organization page of TC-22 (https://iapr-tc22.org/organisation.html) for details.
      2. Candidates who have been members of TC-22 for at least one year.
      3. Candidates who contributed to TC-22 significantly: helping organization of events (RRPR workshop, for example), writing documentation, working on the proceedings of related activities, etc.
      4. The Board will decide if a candidate is eligible.
      5. The reasons to accept or reject an application to join the Board will be given to the candidate.
